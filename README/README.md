# phishing-incident-response
A phishing email impersonating a Microsoft security notification was delivered to employees (Organisation's name withheld)
The organisation is a leading healthcare provider known for its strong use of digital technology. This strength also makes it a prime target for cyber threats. One morning, staff received an email impersonating a Microsoft security notification. It seemed normal but was actually a phishing attempt designed to steal sensitive data such as patient information and financial records. 
The incident revealed that while the systems were secure, employees remained vulnerable to deception. The organization faced both immediate risk and the need for stronger long-term protection against phishing and business email compromise attacks.

PURPOSE:
The project aimed to reduce immediate risks, improve future readiness, and maintain compliance with HIPAA regulations. It also sought to protect Orgnisation’s reputation, ensure continuous operations, and prevent financial losses.

APPROACH:
I worked through seven phases:

Review of suspicious emails.

Validation of domains and sender details.

Analysis of attachments and links for hidden threats.

Examination of network traffic using SIEM tools.

Incident response actions like blocking domains and resetting accounts.

Continuous monitoring for similar threats.

Detailed reporting and documentation.

The investigation was carried out using Tools such as MXToolbox, VirusTotal, Splunk, SpamAssasin, and AbuseipDB.

AWARENESS AND TRAINING:
The technical response notwithstanding, employee education became imperative. Staff were trained to spot common signs of phishing and respond safely. This helped turn the workforce into the first line of defense as should be.

OUTCOME:
The phishing attempts was successfully identified and contained , the chance of future attacks was lowered, staff awareness has increased , and compliance improved . Organization's Network become more stronger, ready to protect both sensitive patients data and trust.
